Understanding Containment Zones

Containment zones are established in various situations to protect public health, safety, and security. Here are some common types:

COVID-19 Containment Zones

Created during the pandemic to control virus spread.

Biological Hazard Zones

Responding to infectious diseases like Ebola.

Environmental Containment Zones

For chemical spills or nuclear accidents.

Wildfire Containment Zones

Designated to control and manage wildfires.

Military or Security Containment Zones

Established for security threats or military operations.

Quarantine Zones

Isolating individuals exposed to contagious diseases.

Natural Disaster Containment Zones

Restricting access after earthquakes or floods.

Biological Research Containment Zones

For hazardous research materials in labs.

Animal Disease Control Zones

Managing outbreaks in agriculture.

Industrial Containment Zones

Ensuring safety in nuclear or chemical industries.

These zones have specific measures and protocols and are managed by relevant authorities.
